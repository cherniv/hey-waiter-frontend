Shalmu Yakubov

Software + Game developer
Summary
I'm a hands-on, qualified programmer with over 15 years experience envisioning, developing and implementing applications, games, synthesizers, websites, language processors, 3D engines, UI libraries for Windows/Linux/Mac, as well as for Android, iOS and web.
My latest passion is a functional approach to development. I also know a lot about code refactoring (yes, Martin Fowler is my hero!), Agile approach helped me and the teams I collaborated with during the year of my work. I am a big fan of extreme programming as well.
I am a convinced advocate for very strict and structured programming. Even in JavaScript I always annotate functions with Google Closure comments and run everything through Facebook Flow JS.
History
My journey began when I was 11 years old: somehow I managed to put my hands on a old Intel 80386 PC (a very rare computer in USSR back then) and a book, called “C programming language”, written by Kernighan and Ritchie, which I learned in 3 months. That summer was the most fascinating time in my childhood. Learning C was an excellent opportunity finally to stop spending all my pocket money playing arcade coin-op machines and to start developing games myself! Little did I know that the programming itself will become the most interesting game I have ever played in my life. My first game was a remake of a legendary arcade "River Rand" by Atari. The rest is just history.
